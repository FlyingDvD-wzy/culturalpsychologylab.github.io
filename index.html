About the Lab

The Cultural Psychology Lab is led by Prof. [Name]. We study how cultural norms, language, and institutions shape psychological processes across diverse populations. Our work spans lab experiments, field studies, and computational models.

Mission

To advance theory and application in cultural psychology through rigorous, reproducible research and open collaboration.

Approach

We use mixed-methods: experiments, longitudinal surveys, qualitative interviews, cross-cultural comparisons, and open data sharing.

Research

Our research streams include:

Culture & Cognition

How cultural context influences reasoning, categorization, and perception.

Moral Psychology

Investigating moral differential modes and universalist vs. particularist ethics.

Identity & Wellbeing

How cultural identities shape mental health and social outcomes.

Selected projects

Moral Differential Mode across Cultures
An experimental-computational project comparing moral judgments in East Asia, Latin America, and Europe.
Open Methods in Cross-cultural Surveys
Developing reproducible methods for large-scale cross-cultural sampling and measurement equivalence.
